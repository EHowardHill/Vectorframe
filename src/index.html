1. Getting Started

Pompedin is a vector-based 2D animation tool. Pompedin separates your drawings into Keyframes on a timeline. When you draw on a blank frame, a new keyframe is automatically created.

Use the Workspace tab to customize the application theme, and the Project tab to adjust your canvas size, frame rate (FPS), and total animation length.

2. Drawing Tools

  • Brush (B): Draws vector strokes. Enable "Pressure" to map pen pressure to stroke width. Enable "Texture" to apply bitmap textures along the vector path.
  • Eraser (E): Removes entire paths or segments that fall under the cursor.
  • Fill (G): Click inside a closed shape to fill it. Toggle "Fill" and "Stroke" in the ribbon to control what gets applied.
  • Hide Edge (H): Click or drag over a line segment to magically hide it while keeping the underlying vector shape intact (great for cel-shading intersections).

3. Selection & Editing

The Select Tool (V) operates in two modes:

  • Object Mode: Click a shape to select it. A bounding box will appear, allowing you to Move, Scale, and Rotate the object.
  • Vertex Mode: Double-click a selected shape to reveal its anchor points. You can drag individual points or adjust their Bézier handles to fine-tune the curve. Press Esc or click empty space to exit.

4. Layers & Timeline

Pompedin supports infinite layers. The timeline dictates when artwork appears.

  • Blank Key (F6): Inserts an empty keyframe at the playhead.
  • Duplicate Key (F7): Copies the current frame's artwork into a new keyframe so you can modify it.
  • Onion Skin: Toggle Onion Skinning in the Tools tab to see faded versions of previous/next frames. Click "+ Add Rule" to configure custom intervals.
  • Right-Click Menu: Right-click any cell on the timeline to Copy, Paste, Insert, or Delete frames.

5. Audio Syncing

You can import an audio file to help with lip-syncing and timing. Go to the Project tab and click "Load Audio". A waveform will appear beneath the timeline ruler. Audio playback scrubs automatically as you play your animation.

6. Exporting

All export options are located in the Export tab.

  • Video (MP4): Renders your animation to a high-quality video file, mixing in your audio track automatically.
  • Animated GIF: Generates a lightweight looping GIF. You can adjust the color count and dithering.
  • Spritesheet: Tiles all frames into a single large PNG image, ideal for game engines.
  • Sequence: Exports every frame as an individual numbered PNG file.

7. Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Space or Middle-Click - Pan Canvas
  • Scroll Wheel - Zoom Canvas
  • Ctrl + Z - Undo
  • Ctrl + Y - Redo
  • Ctrl + S - Save Project
  • Ctrl + C / V - Copy / Paste items (or frames)
  • Delete - Delete selected items
  • Enter - Play / Pause Animation
  • ← / → - Previous / Next Frame
